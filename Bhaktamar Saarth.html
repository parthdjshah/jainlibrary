Shloka 1 - Translation

The radiance of the gem on the head of those who bow to the immortal devotee dispels the darkness of sins. I bow properly to the twin feet of the Jina (Conqueror), which have been a refuge from time immemorial for those who are falling into the ocean of worldly existence.

Shloka 2 - Translation

He who is praised by the lords of celestial worlds, having great intellect and mastery of all forms of speech and knowledge, with hymns that captivate the minds of beings from the three worlds—Even I shall endeavor to praise that First Jina.

Shloka 3 - Translation

Though devoid of intellect, I, without shame, have undertaken to praise the one whose feet are worshipped even by gods. Just as none would try to grasp the reflection of the moon in water leaving aside a child, who else but a fool would dare attempt such a task?

Shloka 4 - Translation

O Ocean of Virtues, moon-bright and radiant! Who is capable of describing your greatness? Even a preceptor among the gods would fall short. Who can cross a storm-tossed ocean, filled with whirlpools and crocodiles, by their arms alone?

Shloka 5 - Translation

Still, O Lord of Sages, due to my devotion, I begin this hymn despite lacking strength. Just like a doe, in love, rushes to protect her calf from a lion without a second thought, I too am moved by devotion alone.

Shloka 6 - Translation

Though I am of little learning and an object of ridicule among the learned, your devotion alone forcibly makes me eloquent. Just like a cuckoo sings sweetly in spring because of the tender mango buds—it is the cause of its sweet voice.

Shloka 7 - Translation

By singing your praise, the sins accumulated over the cycle of births are instantly destroyed in embodied beings. Just like the darkness of night is quickly dispelled when pierced by rays of the sun.

Shloka 8 - Translation

O Lord, I begin this hymn even with my limited intellect, inspired by your grace. Like a dewdrop on a lotus petal shines with the luster of a pearl, so too will my mind gain brilliance through your praise.

Shloka 9 - Translation

Let your praise be, even a simple discussion of your qualities destroys the sins of the world. Just like the sun, from a distance, causes the lotuses in ponds to bloom with its rays.

Shloka 10 - Translation

It is not surprising, O ornament of the world, that beings who praise you with their qualities become equal to you. After all, anyone blessed with greatness makes others equal to himself.

Shloka 11 - Translation

Having seen your ever-delightful form, the eyes find no satisfaction elsewhere. Just as no one would wish to drink salty ocean water after tasting nectar-like milk from the ocean of moonlight.

Shloka 12 - Translation

O Ornament of the Three Worlds! You were created from passionless atoms. Even if others are made of the same atoms, none equal your divine form—there is no second like you.

Shloka 13 - Translation

Where is your face, stealing the gaze of gods, men, and serpents? And where is the blemished moon that pales in daylight, resembling a dried leaf? Your radiance surpasses all comparison.

Shloka 14 - Translation

Your virtues, pure as full-moon rays, transcend the three worlds. Who can obstruct those who have taken shelter in you, the one Lord of all realms, from freely roaming as they wish?

Shloka 15 - Translation

What is surprising if even the beauties of the heavens cannot disturb your mind? Has the peak of Mount Mandara ever shaken, even during the violent winds of the final destruction?

Shloka 16 - Translation

You are like a smokeless lamp with a steady flame, illuminating the entire universe. Like a lamp unmoved by wind, you reveal all, O Lord, the true light of the world.

Shloka 17 - Translation

You never set, nor can Rahu eclipse you. You instantly illuminate the worlds. Even if clouds cover your glory, your brilliance surpasses that of the sun. O Lord of Sages, your radiance is unmatched.

Shloka 18 - Translation

Your radiant lotus-face dispels the great darkness of ignorance eternally. It cannot be veiled by Rahu or clouds. It lights up the world like a previously unseen divine moon.

Shloka 19 - Translation

What use are the moon at night or the sun during day when your moon-like face dispels darkness? In a world already filled with ripe grain, what is the need for rain-laden clouds?

Shloka 20 - Translation

Knowledge shines in you freely as it does not in other deities like Vishnu or Shiva. Just as brilliance appears naturally in gems, but not in broken glass, so too is your unmatched wisdom.

Shloka 21 - Translation

I consider it a blessing that I have seen you, rather than seeing gods like Vishnu and Shiva. Among all I have seen, only your sight brings true satisfaction to my heart. Why look at others, when no one else can captivate the mind like you, O Lord?

Shloka 22 - Translation

Hundreds of women give birth to sons, but none has borne a son like you. Just as all directions can give rise to sunlight, only the East gives birth to the actual sun and its radiance.

Shloka 23 - Translation

Sages declare you to be the Supreme Person, sun-colored, pure, and beyond darkness. Those who truly realize you conquer death. O Sage of sages, there is no other path to liberation but yours.

Shloka 24 - Translation

The wise proclaim you as imperishable, all-pervading, incomprehensible, innumerable, and the first cause. You are Brahma, Ishvara, the infinite, the Lord of Love, the master of yoga, the knower of yoga, the many yet one, the pure form of knowledge.

Shloka 25 - Translation

You alone are the Buddha because of your supreme wisdom. You are Shiva, for you bring auspiciousness to the three worlds. You are the creator, O wise one, as you guide the world to liberation. Clearly, O Lord, you are the Supreme Being.

Shloka 26 - Translation

Salutations to you, O Lord, remover of suffering from all three worlds! Salutations to you, the purest ornament on earth. Salutations to you, the supreme Lord of the three realms. Salutations to you, O Jina, who dries up the ocean of birth and death!

Shloka 27 - Translation

What wonder is there if, being full of boundless virtues, you leave no room for anyone else, O Sage? Those bloated with pride, born of faults and false supports, don’t even dream of reaching you.

Shloka 28 - Translation

Your pure form, shining with rays above the Ashoka tree, appears dazzlingly beautiful. Its brilliant light dispels all darkness, like the blazing sun’s orb beside a cloudbank in the sky.

Shloka 29 - Translation

Seated on a jeweled throne lit by shimmering gem flames, your golden-hued body radiates like the sun rising over the eastern peak, beneath a canopy of sky-rays.

Shloka 30 - Translation

Your radiant body, shining like polished moonlight, is adorned with the gentle waving of pure white chowries. It resembles a golden cliff beside a pure mountain stream under the rising moon.

Shloka 31 - Translation

Your triple umbrella shines with the cool radiance of the moon, placed so high it blocks even the brilliance of the sun's rays. Its beauty is magnified by clusters of pearls and declares your supremacy over the three worlds.

Shloka 32 - Translation

The sound of celestial drums reverberates through the skies, proclaiming your fame. It fills all directions with deep and melodious tones, bringing joy and prosperity to the beings of the three worlds as it heralds the victory of true dharma.

Shloka 33 - Translation

Showers of heavenly flowers from Mandara, Parijata, and other celestial trees descend through a gentle fragrant breeze. Perhaps this is the stream of your divine speech falling from the heavens in blessing.

Shloka 34 - Translation

The halo of your radiance surpasses all luster in the three worlds, outshining even the bright beings. Like the rising sun dissolving the night, your light conquers even the soft glow of the moon.

Shloka 35 - Translation

You are the most sought-after guide to the path of heaven and liberation. You alone are the eloquent teacher of true dharma in all three worlds. Your divine voice is so pure it adapts flawlessly to every language and conveys truth clearly.

Shloka 36 - Translation

Wherever your lotus-like feet touch the earth, they radiate with golden beauty and beams from your toenails. So divine is their presence that even gods imagine blooming lotuses under each step you take.

Shloka 37 - Translation

O Jina, your glory in preaching dharma is unmatched. Just as the sun disperses darkness beyond the reach of even brilliant stars, no other teacher can dispel ignorance as you do.

Shloka 38 - Translation

Even when a maddened elephant, wild with the buzzing of bees and dripping ichor, charges in fury, your devotees remain fearless — for they have taken refuge in you.

Shloka 39 - Translation

The earth is stained with the blood of the elephant pierced in battle, yet even a lion dare not approach one firmly planted in your path. Your protection is such that it halts even the boldest of foes.

Shloka 40 - Translation

At the end of the world, when wildfires rage with gales and sparks ready to devour the universe, even that inferno is extinguished completely by the cool stream of your name's chanting.

Shloka 41 - Translation

The enraged serpent, its eyes red, its throat dark as the cuckoo’s, and its hood raised in fury—such a deadly force is overcome without fear by the one in whose heart your serpent-taming name resides, O Lord.

Shloka 42 - Translation

Even the mightiest armies of kings, with their terrifying noise of horses and elephants, are shattered like darkness at dawn—when struck by the rays of the rising sun—so too are they broken by the power of your name's praise.

Shloka 43 - Translation

In the most fearsome battles, warriors eager to cross rivers of blood from wounded elephants achieve victory over even invincible foes—if they have taken refuge in the forest of your lotus feet.

Shloka 44 - Translation

Those crossing the terrifying ocean filled with crocodile whirlpools, tossing fish, and burning submarine fires—by merely remembering you—cross over without fear, like boats riding the waves.

Shloka 45 - Translation

Those afflicted by incurable diseases, weighed down and near death, attain radiant beauty like Cupid himself when their bodies are touched by the dust of your lotus feet.

Shloka 46 - Translation

People bound from head to foot in heavy chains and crushed by iron fetters are instantly freed from fear and bondage by constantly remembering the mantra of your name.

Shloka 47 - Translation

The wise one who studies this hymn dedicated to you sees the destruction of all fears—be they from wild beasts, fire, serpents, war, or even the vast ocean of worldly bondage.

Shloka 48 - Translation

This garland of your praise, woven with virtues and devotion, strung with beautiful, multicolored words—when worn constantly around the neck by anyone, wealth and fortune come humbly to serve them.
